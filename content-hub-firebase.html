      Industry & market trends

      Researched Jul 2026

      Grounded in current LinkedIn/B2B research relevant to Mediashock's audience (Communications Directors and technical B2B leaders), industry (corporate video & event storytelling), and market (APAC, led out of Singapore). This is a point-in-time snapshot from external research, not something the dashboard computes from your own data — ask again periodically to refresh it.

      • Post through personal profiles, not just the company page. Company page organic reach has fallen to roughly 1.6% of followers on average (down 60–66% since 2024), while personal profiles get 5–8x the engagement and up to 561% wider reach when employees reshare company content. Have founders and team members re-post flagship pieces from their own profiles instead of relying on the company page alone. Personal vs. company page reach, 2026 →
      • Lean further into native video. Native video gets ~5x the engagement of static posts and is shared roughly 20x more — and it's the format Mediashock is actually built to produce. LinkedIn: video isn't just content →
      • For text-only posts, keep them plain and native. Simple native text is currently outperforming carousels for reach (~28% higher reach, ~34% better engagement) — save carousels for genuine step-by-step or portfolio content rather than defaulting to them. LinkedIn algorithm update, June 2026 →
      • For event promotion, lead with expertise, not the registration link. Buyers now discover events through posts and people they already trust rather than announcement posts, and links off-platform (like registration pages) reduce distribution under the current algorithm — this matches Mediashock's own Content Choreography thesis. LinkedIn: turning events into pipeline →
      • Write for depth, not likes. The algorithm now weights an engagement "depth score" and comment quality over comment count — ending a post with a genuine question drives ~77% more comments than average. LinkedIn thought leadership, 2026 →
      • Avoid generic AI-toned copy. LinkedIn's 2026 update actively suppresses generic AI-generated posts and rewards unique perspective and lived experience — the exact advantage a storytelling agency should be leaning on rather than smoothing away. Why generic AI content kills reach →

      Reference discussion posts on these exact trends (from other practitioners, not Mediashock): 2026 B2B content & marketing trends · B2B events are evolving · B2B thought leadership research. I couldn't reliably surface specific viral posts from comparable video/event agencies — LinkedIn's own posts aren't well-indexed by web search — so these are analysis posts about the trends rather than examples of the format itself.
